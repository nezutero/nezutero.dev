Since ditching my smartphone the way I communicate has changed, I want to express my feelings about the way we communicate nowadays, more precisely:

- What I hate about it
- Why it's overwheming
- How to deal with it

So, first of all, yes, I finally ditched my smartphone, but not in the way you might think. The thing about I was dreaming and for which I was preparing for for months finally happend. This is not the topic I want to put emphasis on in this post, nevertheless I cannot wait to share my journey, so I will write another post about the long waiting switch I made recently.

**The "What I Hate about it" part**

Well, I guess many of us at least once felt overwhemed because of constant notofications, whatever form it takes. I am not an exception here; I'm done, constantly feeling tired of it and overwhemed by messages I receive (emails, personal chats, group chats). Not that I have that many friends or people who contact me that often, but I have a few of those, and almost everyday someone thanks god texts me; and not calls, thought I have a few people prefer to get to me by calling, but this is a whole another topic. What I don't like about calls is that it's sync communication method, it requires your presence, but most importantly -- attention. Imagine yourself in the deep focus state, doing something important for you: learning math, programming, writing, reading, whatever, and there comes a moment at which you become no longer focused on that thing, because someone is calling you. Horrible. That's why I don't like calls -- they are disturbing. Don't get me wrong, i like having calls with my familly or friends, but they are scheduled/preplanned, so it is a different thing.

Messages are better because of their asyncronous nature; they don't ask for you immediately, thus a person can reply at whatever moment is convinient. But the reality is that in our world, full of distractions and constant notifications, being constantly availible has been a "standard", first of all which I hate by the way, and sencondly which is fake, because there is no such a standard, just lies and expectations of other people, which indeed are just their expectations formed in the first place under the pressure of other people, which can actually only exist in their head.

I undestand that unread messages or long responces can be seen as ignorance or not being respectful, but before making such statements, it is crucial to have a responce on this question:

- Am I being respectful to others or am I being disrespectful to myself?

Maybe a bit confusing, but sounds cool. What I mean, is that a lot of us are being respectful to others by giving up respect for ourselves, which is not good, and not healthy at all. In a long run this behaviour makes you live not for yourself, but for others, with the goal of meeting others' expectations. This trap is real, in fact this how our psyche works; people pleasing is a survival mechanism. Nevertheless, it is super important to be consious about it, undestand that it is ok, and every time you make a choice between yourself and not yourself. Super simplified, I know, to the point where if seen just from one perspectice might be seen like others are enemies. That's not true, they are your friends, all of them. What I wanna say is that I respecting yourself, choosing and prioritizing what you want to do and how you want to live is crucial for one's hapiness, which is related to all areas of our life.
